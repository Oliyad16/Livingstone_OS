# DC CBE Set-Aside Playbook — finding the contracts only CBEs can win

Livingstone is a **certified DC CBE (Certified Business Enterprise)**. That cert
only pays off on **sheltered-market / set-aside** solicitations — and those are
**not visible to public web search**. They live behind the logged-in OCP
eSourcing vendor portal. This playbook is the exact setup so those contracts come
to you instead of you hunting for them.

> Why this matters: external research tools (and our daily research agent) can see
> RFPMart and SAM.gov, but **cannot see DC sheltered-market RFPs** (`DCKA-2026-*`
> set-asides where non-CBEs literally cannot bid). The portal login is the unlock.

---

## One-time setup (do this once)

### 1. Register / log into OCP eSourcing
- Go to **https://ocp.dc.gov/service/ocp-solicitations** → "OCP eSourcing" (the
  OC Solicitation Gateway / vendor portal).
- Register as a **vendor/respondent** if you haven't. Use the entity that holds
  the CBE certification (same legal name as on the DSLBD certificate).
- Confirm your **CBE certification is linked** to the vendor profile (so you show
  up as eligible for sheltered-market notices).

### 2. Set your commodity codes (so alerts match our work)
DC uses **NIGP commodity codes**. Add these to the vendor profile so the portal
emails you when matching solicitations post:
- **920** — IT / data-processing services (the primary one)
- **915 / 918** — Communications / consulting & management services
- **961 / 962** — Advertising, marketing, public relations (for the DC Lottery /
  comms-style RFPs)
- **208 / 209** — Software / web (where listed)

### 3. Turn ON respondent alerts / "follow" notifications
- Enable email notifications for **new solicitations** and **amendments** on the
  codes above.
- This is the whole point: sheltered-market RFPs then arrive in your inbox the
  day they post.

### 4. Register on the sibling portals (same idea, different DC bodies)
- **DGS OpenGov** (since Jul 2024, all DGS solicitations >$100K):
  https://dgs.dc.gov/page/active-existing-solicitations — set up a vendor account
  + alerts.
- **DGS "Propel DC"** — DGS's CBE/SBE set-aside initiative; check the current
  registration window (it has run cohort-based intake): https://dgs.dc.gov/page/propel-dc
- **DC Housing Authority** e-procurement ("Housing Agency Marketplace"):
  https://ha.internationaleprocurement.com/requests.html?company_id=506
- **DC Lottery / OLG** — has its own CBE & Small Business Development Program;
  watch for the annual advertising/digital-marketing RFP re-post:
  https://dclottery.com/olg/cbe-and-small-business-development-program

### 5. Subscribe to the forecast feed (pre-position before the RFP drops)
- **DC Contracts & Procurement Transparency Portal** publishes solicitations,
  awards, AND **forecasts**: https://contracts.ocp.dc.gov/solicitations/search
- The forecast tells you what's coming so you can prep a response in advance.

---

## What to watch for (so you recognize a real CBE set-aside)

- **Solicitation number prefixes:** `DCKA-2026-*` (OCP central services — most of
  our web/digital/IT lane), `GAGA-2026-*` (DC Public Schools), `DCAM-*` (DGS
  construction/AE — usually not us).
- **"Sheltered market" / "set-aside for CBEs"** language = non-CBEs can't bid =
  your best odds. Contracts roughly **$100K–$250K** are frequently CBE-only.
- **NAICS that map to us:** 541511 (web/custom programming), 541512 (systems
  design), 541519 (other IT), 541810/541850 (advertising/display), 541613
  (marketing consulting), 519130 (web portals), 541430 (graphic design).
- On open-market bids, CBE still adds **preference points** + agencies have CBE
  subcontracting mandates — so being registered helps even when it's not a hard
  set-aside.

---

## The weekly habit (5 minutes)

1. Log into **OCP eSourcing**; scan new `DCKA-2026-*` services solicitations.
2. Check **DGS OpenGov** for anything >$100K in our codes.
3. Skim the **transparency-portal forecast** for upcoming items to pre-position.
4. Anything that fits → add it as a deal room in the dashboard (Government
   workspace) and start the response.

> The daily research agent (`gov-cbe-daily-research`, 7am) covers the *public*
> sources (SAM.gov, RFPMart, agency sites). This portal habit covers the
> *gated* sources it can't reach. Together = full coverage.
